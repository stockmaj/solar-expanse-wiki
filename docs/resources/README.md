# Resources

Resources are produced by facilities, shipped between worlds in spacecraft
cargo holds, traded on the marketplace, and consumed in construction. Three
types exist:

- **Normal** — physical materials, the bulk of the economy.
- **Energy** — power; produced and consumed in real time, with limited storage in batteries.
- **Human** — colonists; produced over time by habitats and consumed by jobs.

## Reading the table

- **License (Earth, $/t)** is the per-tonne fee Earth charges for extracting each resource. Earth is the only body in the shipped data with non-zero license fees — every other body's `licenseFeePerTonne` map is empty.
- **Market base ($/t)** is the starting clearing-price anchor used by the global market; supply and demand move actual prices around it.
- **Boiling pt.** is the effective boiling temperature at the selected body's atmospheric pressure, computed with the game's own Clausius-Clapeyron formula (`TerraformationConfig.cs` line 599). Defaults to Earth. Use the selector above to change the body or enter a pressure directly (in Earth atmospheres). Resources without phase data show —.
- **Producers** and **Consumers** are pulled from each facility's structured production data (`refinerData`, `energyProductionData`, `resourcesToMine`, `byproducts`) — not from tooltip text — so refineries don't get mis-credited as producing their inputs. Per-day rates aren't extractable from the static descriptors; the in-game tooltip remains the source of truth for rate numbers.
