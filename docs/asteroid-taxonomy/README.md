# Asteroid Taxonomy

Each asteroid has a class — Carbon, Dark, Helium-3, Metal, or Stone.
When you mine a deposit on an asteroid, the resource you actually extract
is rolled from the table below, grouped by the deposit's quality tier
(High, Mid, or Low).  Probabilities within each tier sum to 100%.

## Carbon Asteroid

| Tier | Resource | Probability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Carbon | 100% |
| Low | Metals | 45% |
| Low | Water | 45% |
| Low | Silicon | 10% |

## Dark Asteroid

| Tier | Resource | Probability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Water | 100% |
| Low | Carbon | 90% |
| Low | Silicon | 5% |
| Low | Nitrogen | 5% |

## Helium-3 Asteroid

| Tier | Resource | Probability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Water | 50% |
| High | Carbon | 50% |
| Mid | Nitrogen | 5% |
| Mid | Noble Gas | 95% |
| Low | Helium-3 | 100% |

## Metal Asteroid

| Tier | Resource | Probability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Rare Metals | 100% |
| Low | Fissiles | 30% |
| Low | Metals | 65% |
| Low | Water | 5% |

## Stone Asteroid

| Tier | Resource | Probability |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High | Silicon | 95% |
| High | Metals | 5% |
| Low | Rare Metals | 40% |
| Low | Fissiles | 35% |
| Low | Carbon | 25% |
